# Nest welcome sequence

Three emails sent to new "Join the Nest" subscribers. Voice is the site's: cozy,
unhurried, book-lover warmth. First person — these come from Ayla, not from a brand.

**Status:** copy only. No sending platform is wired up yet (see *Sending* below).

**On the discount code:** the launch discount is deliberately *promised but not coded*.
Email 1 says it's locked in; the actual code goes out in the launch email. Don't invent
a code here — it has to match whatever the shop honors on day one.

**Before sending, check:** the launch date below says "August 15." If that slips,
fix it here *and* in `index.html` (hero badge + FAQ) so the two never disagree.

## Email 2 — The story (sends ~3 days after Email 1)

**Subject:** Every great adventure begins with a single chapter
**Preheader:** Mine started somewhere I didn't expect.

Hi again,

I promised you the story, so here it is.

Dragon Ink and Thread didn't start with a business plan. It started after I became a
mother, in the middle of postpartum depression, when I was searching for something that
felt like *me* again. I needed somewhere to slow down and work with my hands — to make one
beautiful thing and know I'd made it.

The hobby became a passion. The passion became this.

Books got me through, the way they always have. From the first time I opened *Twilight* to
getting lost in *Throne of Glass*, they reminded me that ordinary moments can hold
adventure, comfort, and hope. That's what I'm trying to put into everything I sew — the
feeling of a chapter you don't want to end.

It's why nothing here is rushed. Everything is made in small batches, made to order. When a
fabric sells out, that print retires and becomes part of the shop's history, making room for
the next chapter.

You don't have to leave wonder behind just because you've grown up.

→ [Read the whole story](https://www.dragoninkandthread.com/#about)

— Ayla

## Sending

Not yet wired up. The site's forms POST to Web3Forms, which is a form-to-email relay — it
notifies dragoninkandthread@gmail.com on each signup but does **not** keep a mailing list.
Free-tier submissions are dropped after 30 days, and its autoresponder is a paid Pro feature
that can only reply to a submission, never broadcast to past subscribers.

These three emails will be sent from **Buttondown** (chosen 2026-07-10), which will also send
the launch announcement and the discount code. Until the account exists and the forms are
repointed, the Nest list effectively lives in a Gmail inbox.

**When migrating:** back-fill the existing subscribers out of Gmail first — those signup
notifications are the only record of them.
